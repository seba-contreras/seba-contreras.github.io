Biography

Hey!

I am Sebastian (Seba) Contreras, PhD student at the Priesemann group working on dynamical systems and disease spread. Using mathematical modelling, I search to uncover general principles in the dynamics of infectious diseases and gain quantitative insights on the controllability of an outbreak. My recent work focuses on studying non-pharmaceutical interventions, competition and coinfection dynamics between diseases, and the complex interplay between information and disease spread (pandemic–infodemic).

Besides my primary research, I have several active collaborations with researchers from various areas (e.g., theoretical ecology, protein engineering, cancer research, social scientists, and geostatisticians). If you feel like your research could use some extra modelling, let’s get in touch!

Featured Publications

Generalized property-based encoders and digital signal processing facilitate predictive tasks in protein engineering
Here we generalize property-based encoders for amino acid sequences and demonstrate that they substantially increase ML models' performance in predictive tasks in Protein Engineering. These encoders arise from semantically-consistent groups of properties within the AAIndex database and, combined with Fourier Transforms, increase the performance metrics across various tasks. All these results offer simple ways to increase the performance of general and complex predictive tasks in protein engineering without increasing their complexity.
David Medina-Ortiz, Seba Contreras, Juan Amado-Hinojosa, Jorge Torres-Almonacid, Juan A. Asenjo, Marcelo Navarrete, Alvaro Olivera-Nappa
Interplay Between Risk Perception, Behavior, and COVID-19 Spread
Pharmaceutical and non-pharmaceutical interventions (NPIs) have been crucial for controlling COVID-19. They are complemented by voluntary health-protective behavior, building a complex interplay between risk perception, behavior, and disease spread. We studied how voluntary health-protective behavior and vaccination willingness impact the long-term dynamics. Altogether, our work highlights the importance of appropriate mandatory NPIs to maximise the impact of individual voluntary actions in pandemic control.
Philipp Dönges, Joel Wagner, Seba Contreras, Emil N. Iftekhar, Simon Bauer, Sebastian B. Mohr, Jonas Dehning, André Calero Valdez, Mirjam Kretzschmar, Michael Mäs, Kai Nagel, Viola Priesemann
